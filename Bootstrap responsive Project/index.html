About the Farm

We are affiliated with the extensive New Community Project network and have strong ties to the activities at Vine and Fig, an urban educational center and living laboratory. Amid our work, you'll frequently see us joyfully dancing and singing, or relishing a delightful farm-cooked lunch by the creek. We embrace everyone, striving to establish a secure and courteous environment where all individuals are valued and respected.

Our Story

Muddy Creek Carbon Farm

The Jubilee Climate Farm was established in March 2021 with the goal of actively participating in the worldwide initiative to preserve the well-being and sustainability of our planet for generations to come. Additionally, we offer a platform for individuals to engage in farming alongside us within our community garden. Our mission is to foster constructive approaches to address climate change, promote food justice, uphold food sovereignty, advocate for land equity, and empower one another mutually.


Our Mission

Our aim is to establish a blueprint for revolutionizing agricultural systems, turning them into pivotal solutions for the climate crisis. Simultaneously, we seek to offer recent immigrants, students, local farmers, and anyone interested in collaboration the opportunities for land access, empowerment, and education.

What We Do

Carbon Farming

Implementation of a suite of crops and agricultural practices that sequester carbon in the soil and in perennial biomass

Community Gardens

A space for farmers who do not presently have access to land - recent refugees, immigrants, and anyone else looking to collaborate!

Small Farms Carbon Alliance

Bring together small farmers and community organizers locally and around the world to share experiences, knowledge, and resources in order to develop best practices to increase carbon sequestration on their farms and in their communities

Riparian Buffer System

I'm Jacob, a senior at Bridgewater College and one of the interns here at Jubilee! So far, my work at the farm has been centered on the research, design, and planting of the riparian buffer along Muddy Creek - mostly working on the design of the buffer and spacing the trees so that they have the best chance at surviving. We started planting last week and should be finishing up this Friday, so the next step is figuring out the most effective maintenance to make sure the saplings keep growing and the buffer zone becomes a productive part of the farm. I also work on other projects at Jubilee, primarily the silvopasture swales, and helped build the chicken coop!I also work at a food pantry garden attached to Bridgewater College, and have been working on adapting some of the agricultural techniques we use at the farm - raised beds, polyculture, and intercropping - to the space we have available at BC!

Hi, my name is Charlotte Terry and I am working on Silvopasture Design and Research. The Effect of Carbon Farming Practices on Plant Yield and Soil Health

Anna Sortore and Tyler Aston
Advisor: Dr. Jennifer Coffman
"For our capstone, we are working with the Jubilee Climate Farm to study the effects of different cover crops and farming practices on the amount of carbon sequestered from the atmosphere. We will be planting and harvesting multiple plant varieties throughout the year, while making weekly observations and collecting data to be analyzed in the ISAT Environment and Waypoint Laboratories. With this data, we hope to find a relationship between carbon sequestered and crop yield, to potentially help provide the local community with healthy food sources, while also sequestering the maximum amount of carbon from the atmosphere."
